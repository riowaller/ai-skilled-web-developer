## Meeting Summary: CIT Curriculum & Sequencing Update
**Date:** Wednesday, May 20, 2026
**Participants:** Rio Waller, Dennis , Breanna Rayburn , Lori and Drew 

---

### 1. Program Sequence & Prerequisites
The committee reviewed changes to the two-year course cycle, focusing on aligning student schedules with optimal learning progression.
*   **Third-Semester Shifting:** CIT 28 (Data Analytics) is officially being moved to the third semester. Students will take it alongside a core programming choice. 

---

### 2. Program Naming & Market Sentiment Around AI
Rio updated the team on administrative approval regarding the proposed curriculum names.
*   **Administrative Support:** Liz (Administration) reviewed the curriculum documentation and praised the program good model for true AI integration rather than just layering it superficially over legacy materials.
*   **Shifting Public Sentiment:** While the group initially favored "**AI Skilled Web Development**" (7 votes), Rio and Breanna pointed out that public and academic sentiment regarding the "AI" label is growing increasingly hostile (citing instances of AI references being booed at recent college graduations).
*   **The Aging Dilemma:** Concerns persist regarding whether "AI" or "Emerging Web Technologies" will age poorly or look like a passing trend. No final decision has been made; Rio will monitor sentiment over the summer and seek guidance from the advisory board.

---

### 3. Elimination of Electives (The Cafeteria Model)
The team discussed dropping the traditional "cafeteria plate" of elective options in favor of a tightly locked core requirements list.
*   **Eliminating Lower-Value Options:** The team discusses approaches.
*   **Adding Vibe Coding & Python to the Core:** 
    *   **CIT 60** will be re-imagined as an 18-week, language-agnostic **Vibe Coding / Computer Concepts** class placed in the first semester core alongside Intro to AI and Web Developmen (CIT82) (front 9) and HTML/CSS (CIT 85) Back 9 . This will teach core conceptual architectures to prepare students for JavaScript (CIT 93) in semester two.
    *   **Python** will officially become a required core class rather than an option, as it provides a cleaner foundational environment for server-side structures.
*   **Agentic AI Transition:** Rio announced plans to transform the existing Framework course (CIT84) into an **Agentic AI** framework management class.
*   **Low Enrollment Justification:** Dennis pointed out that shifting these from optional to required core courses gives faculty massive leverage when justifying low-enrollment sections to administration, as students *must* pass them to graduate.

---

### 4. Administrative Protocols & Regional Outreach
*   **Regional Alignment:** Rio has reached out to web development faculty at the **Madera**, **Reedley** and **Clovis** campuses to evaluate class equivalencies and strengthen curriculum change documentation.
*   **CIP/TOPS Code Update:** Rio is investigating a switch to an **Informatics** classification for the program to preserve access to Perkins CTE funding. She will coordinate with Kelly O'Rourke to navigate potential administrative backdoor paths.
*   **Standardizing DEI Language:** The department is evaluating a push to standardize Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion (DEI) language across course descriptions while preserving the instructor’s ability to specify course-level context.
