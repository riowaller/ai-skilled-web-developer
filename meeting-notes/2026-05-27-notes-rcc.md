Here is a summary of your meeting with Naun Garcia and Daniel From Reedley Community College regarding the integration of AI into the Web Development curriculum at Fresno City College.

### Meeting Overview
The discussion centered on how community college programs (specifically at Fresno City College and Reedley College) are adapting their Computer Information Technology (CIT) and Web Development curricula to address the rapid rise of AI. The group shared strategies for updating course structures, maintaining academic integrity, and ensuring students learn core fundamental skills rather than just relying on AI code generation.

---

### Curriculum Redesign Strategy
* **Program Overhaul:** You detailed the transition of the Web Developer program to a fully OER (Open Educational Resources) model, potentially renaming it "AI Skilled Web Developer" (though the name is still being debated due to longevity concerns).
* **Course Sequence:** The new sequence includes an Intro to AI and Web Development, an HTML/CSS "mechanic shop" class (where AI is used to understand code, not generate it), a language-agnostic Coding Concepts class, JavaScript, SQL, and an Agentic AI/Node.js capstone.
* **Naun's Approach:** Reedley College is currently brainstorming their updates, planning to introduce a foundational AI concepts class (similar to IS15) while sprinkling AI usage throughout existing modules. 

---

### Combating AI Cheating & Ensuring Comprehension
* **Technical Interviews:** You shared your highly successful approach of conducting 30-minute live coding or code-walkthrough interviews with students (particularly in JavaScript and SQL). 
* **Logistics:** These are scheduled around the 9th week using office hours, with incentives for students who book early. 
* **Results:** This method achieved a 77% success rate in JavaScript, serving as a reliable way to assess true understanding and deter AI plagiarism. Naun expressed strong interest in adopting this method.

---

### Tools & Resources Shared
* **Custom AI Canvas Tool:** You discussed an AI tool you developed for Canvas that tracks student attempts and acts as a tutor rather than just giving answers, though getting district funding remains a major hurdle.
* **GitHub Codespaces:** You recommended this as a frictionless, cloud-based VS Code environment that includes built-in SQLite. This solves hardware limitations for dual-enrollment students or those using Chromebooks.
* **Harvard CS50:** You utilize Harvard's CS50 SQL content, requiring students to code along and commit their work via Git to prove their understanding.

---

### Shared Challenges & Concerns
* **The AI "Crutch":** Both you and Naun expressed concern that students might use AI to bypass learning foundational logic and problem-solving, which could break the developmental ladder from junior to senior developer.
* **Administrative Disconnect:** There is mutual frustration regarding district-level funding blockades for software and the lack of understanding from decision-makers about the continuing necessity of web development education.
* **Industry Uncertainty:** The group acknowledged the rapid, unpredictable evolution of AI tools (like ChatGPT and Claude), making it difficult to "future-proof" course names and content.
