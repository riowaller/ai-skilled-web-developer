## 📋 Meeting Overview & Key Discussions

### 1. Requisites & Pathway Preparation
* **Prerequisite Reform:** The team discussed addressing outdated prerequisites. There is a focus on updating requisites for core courses to better prepare students for the technical rigor and evolving demands of the job market.
* **Alternative Pathways:** Discussion on offering alternative routes or "leveling" opportunities for students to gain necessary skills if they do not follow the traditional prerequisite chain, ensuring flexibility without sacrificing student readiness.

### 2. Electives & Dynamic Curriculum
* **Dynamic Options:** The team explored a more flexible elective system. The goal is to allow students to choose "Options" (such as Python, Java, or Machine Learning) based on their specific career interests and current skill levels.
* **Curriculum Agility:** Acknowledged that elective offerings should be responsive to the evolving tech landscape and diverse student goals.

### 3. AI Integration & Collaborative Development
* **AI-Related Projects:** Suggestions were made to weave specific AI-related projects and technology across the curriculum, ensuring students are "building" and "problem-solving" with modern tools.
* **Collaborative Environment:** The team highlighted the importance of involving adjunct faculty in the development process and creating a more collaborative learning environment that includes student input.

### 4. Strategic Alignment
* **Industry Readiness:** Continued focus on updating the curriculum to align with modern workplace demands, specifically regarding technology integration and specialized workflows.
