## 📋 Meeting Overview & Key Discussions

### 1. Program Vision & Core Changes
* **Overview of Changes:** Presented the transition from traditional Web Development to the "AI-Skilled" model.
* **AI Web Workflows (CIT 94):** The addition of this course was **generally accepted** as the program capstone (4 units).
* **The "AI" Brand:** Discussion on whether "AI" should explicitly appear in the program title or individual course names. Concerns were raised about long-term naming relevancy.
* **Vibe Coding:** Explored how "vibe coding" (high-level, intent-based development) fits. Suggestion made to offer a **"Game Vibe Coding"** section to increase student engagement and lower the entry barrier.

### 2. Pedagogy & Quality Control
* **Technical Interviews:** General consensus that these align with industry hiring practices.
    * **Concerns:** Discussion on the logistics of how these are handled (grading consistency, time per student, and scalability).
* **Fundamentals vs. AI:** Strong emphasis on ensuring students learn coding fundamentals first.
* **Test-Driven Development (TDD):** Discussion on the critical importance of TDD. Aligning with industry needs means students must be able to verify AI-generated code through rigorous testing.

### 3. Prerequisites & Student Success
* **The JavaScript Barrier:** Discussion regarding students taking **CIT 93 (JavaScript)** without prior coding experience, often just to satisfy graduation or CSU transfer requirements.
* **Requisite Benefits/Challenges:** Debated the impact of enforcing prerequisites versus maintaining open access. There is a need to balance student readiness with program completion rates.

### 4. Marketing & Industry Alignment
* **Marketability:** Discussion on how to effectively market these changes to students and the community.
* **Industry Needs:** Continued focus on ensuring the curriculum produces "work-ready" developers who can navigate modern, AI-augmented workplaces.
