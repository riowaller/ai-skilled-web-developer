### 1. Curriculum & Course Title Debates
The team discussed the naming and structure of a new course focused on modern programming methods.
*   **"Vibe Coding":** Dennis proposed "Programming Concepts with Vibe Coding." There was significant debate regarding the term "Vibe Coding." While it captures the current trend of using AI prompts to generate code, some (Brianna and Rick) expressed concern that it might have a negative or "unprofessional" connotation in academia and the workforce.
*   **Course Content:** The course aims to be **language agnostic**, allowing students to choose their own programming language while focusing on core concepts like control structures, variables, and memory allocation.
*   **Textbook Requirements:** The curriculum committee requires a formal textbook. It was agreed that a standard "Programming Concepts" textbook (potentially OER) could be used, with "Vibe Coding" as the specific "flavor" or methodology of the instruction.

### 3. Prerequisite Restructuring
Brianna proposed a significant change to the prerequisite funnel to ensure students are technically prepared for advanced courses:
*   **The Problem:** Students often enter JavaScript (CIT 93) or HTML/CSS (CIT 85) without basic file management, command line, or GitHub skills, leading to high drop rates.
*   **The Proposal:** Add **CIT 82** (Introduction to Web Development) as a prerequisite (or "OR" option) for advanced courses.
*   **The "Big Door" Strategy:** Keep CIT 82 with **no prerequisites** to allow easy entry, but require it (or equivalents like CIT 15 or CIT 48A) for the rest of the sequence.
*   **Equivalency Concerns:** Matt noted that CIT 48A is more rigorous and hands-on than CIT 15, but the team agreed that having multiple entry paths (15, 48A, or 82) is better than having no prerequisites at all.

---

### 4. "Supportive Entry" / Readiness Bridge
The team discussed a "Supportive Entry" model (essentially a voluntary "Boot Camp") to help students prepare:
*   **Structure:** An optional Canvas module or workshop open 3–4 weeks before the semester starts.
*   **Goal:** To help students self-assess and learn basic skills (GitHub, VS Code, File Systems) before the "heavy" first week of classes begins.
*   **Implementation:** This will be a summer project for Rio and Rick to develop for their respective courses.
