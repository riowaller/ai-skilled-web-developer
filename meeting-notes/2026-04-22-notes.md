## 📋 Meeting Overview & Key Discussions

### **1. Major Curriculum Shift: "Supportive Entry" Model**
The group proposed a "radical" change to remove traditional hard prerequisites for all core web developer courses.
* **The Model:** Instead of blocking enrollment, students enter a **Supportive Entry** phase. Upon enrollment, the Canvas course is published early, providing a "Module 0" or "Quick Start" containing skill audits, pre-tests, and "Bridge" materials.
* **Implementation:** The first week of class will focus on validating these skills. If students identify gaps, they are funneled to specific electives or resources to "level up" without the risk of a failing grade.
* **Goal:** To reduce the high drop rate in courses like **CIT 82** and **CIT 85**, where students often realize too late they lack the foundational skills (like file management or browser tag handling) to succeed.

---

### **2. Program Rebranding & Name Change**
The team debated how to rename the program to reflect the inclusion of AI while ensuring the title remains relevant as the technology matures.
* **Current Proposals:**
    * *Full Stack Web Development with Applied AI*
    * *AI Integrated Web Development*
    * *Web Development with AI Workflows*
    * *Modern Web Development and Innovation*
* **Concerns:** There was a significant discussion about "AI" being a buzzword that might carry negative social sentiment or age poorly. Some suggested sticking to "Modern Web Development" to encompass Git, Node, and AI as a standard toolkit.
* **Next Steps:** The group will conduct market research on job postings and vote on a final name in two weeks.

---

### **3. Course-Specific Updates & Technical Requirements**
* **CIT 28 (SQL):** Proposed name change to "Intro to SQL" or "SQL" to better reflect the course content (moving away from "Client-Server Database").
* **Technical Interviews:** The program will integrate technical interviews for core classes (**CIT 28, 93,** and potentially **94**) to prepare students for industry standards.
* **Machine Learning (ML):** Decision made to remove the ML course from the web developer option for now. The consensus was that ML requires a heavy math/statistics background that isn't currently supported by the "Supportive Entry" model.

---

### **4. AI Integration Strategy**
* **AI as a Tool:** AI will be treated as an "integrated tool" rather than the sole focus. It will be used as a tutor in some courses and for workflow automation in others (the "Capstone" course).
* **OER Commitment:** The team is prioritizing **Open Educational Resources (OER)**. They declined to include certain existing AI courses because they aren't currently OER-compliant.

---

### **5. Administrative & Compliance Considerations**
* **Financial Aid & Transfer:** Lori will investigate if removing prerequisites impacts unit counts for financial aid or transfer agreements with CSUs.
* **Taxonomy of Programs:** The group is looking into changing the program's "TOP code" to align with new state classifications for AI-related fields.
