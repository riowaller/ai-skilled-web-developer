## 🛣️ The "Student Success" Map (Recommended Sequence)
To address concerns about the "JavaScript Barrier," the workgroup recommends the following semester-based pathway:

### **Semester 1: The Foundations**
* **CIT 82:** Essential for AI literacy and prompt engineering.
* **CIT 85:** Essential for manual structural authoring.
* *Goal: Build the "Mechanical Shop" basics before moving to logic.*

### **Semester 2: The Engineering Core**
* **CIT 93:** Requires the web foundations from Semester 1.
* **CIT 28:** Can be taken concurrently with CIT 93.
* **CIT 94:** The final capstone for AI integration.

---

## 🧠 Workgroup Rationale for Prerequisite Reform
1. **Closing the JavaScript Gap:** By requiring CIT 82 before CIT 93, we ensure students aren't just learning "syntax" in a vacuum, but are applying logic to the web architectures they already understand.
2. **AI-Ready Options:** Option courses (Python/Machine Learning) require a baseline of computational thinking. Updating advisories prevents students from entering high-unit electives without the necessary technical "vocabulary."
3. **Open-Entry "Hooks":** We will maintain courses like **CIT 60 (Game Vibe Coding)** as open-entry to serve as high-engagement interest builders for new students.
