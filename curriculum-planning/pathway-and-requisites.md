## 🛣️ The "Student Success" Map (Recommended Sequence)
*Note: **CIT 48A** is the preferred foundational entry point for students intending to complete the full AI-Skilled Developer track.*

### **Foundation: Infrastructure (Term 0)**
* **CIT 48A:** IT+ Fundamentals (Hardware, OS, Networking, Security).

### **Semester 1: The Foundations**
* **CIT 82:** Introduction to AI and Web Development.
* **CIT 85:** Applied HTML and CSS.
* *Goal: Establish AI literacy, manual authoring, and file-system mastery.*

### **Semester 2: Programming Fundamentals**
* **CIT 93:** JavaScript.
* *Goal: Focus exclusively on logic, TDD, and asynchronous programming.*

### **Semester 3: Advanced Data & Integration**
* **CIT 28:** SQL (Data Modeling).
* **CIT 94:** AI Web Workflows (Capstone).

## 🧠 Workgroup Rationale: CIT 15 vs. CIT 48A

### 1. Technical Depth
While CIT 15 provides a broad overview of computer concepts, **CIT 48A (IT+ Fundamentals)** focuses on hardware, networking topologies, and security. For an AI-Skilled developer, understanding networking and operating systems is critical for future work in deployment and LLM orchestration (CIT 94/97).

### 2. The "Post-82" Technical Handshake
Courses following CIT 82 assume students have mastered foundational workflow tools. The **Success Audit** identifies gaps for students who may have bypassed the preferred CIT 48A/82 route.

* **Git & Version Control:** Mandatory for CIT 93, 28, and 94. 
* **Terminal Literacy:** Navigating directories and running scripts—skills explicitly introduced in CIT 48A.
* **Supportive Entry (The Bridge):** Students entering via the CIT 15 route will be directed to "Bridge" content in Module 0 to catch up on the OS and Terminal skills found in CIT 48A.

### 3. The CSU Fresno Transfer Dilemma
To protect CIT 93 enrollment for transfer students, the program keeps CIT 93 accessible via CIT 15, but uses the **Success Audit** to ensure those students skill-up on the "Mechanical Shop" standards before the first lab.
