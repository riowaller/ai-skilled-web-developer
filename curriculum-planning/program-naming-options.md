# Program & Course Naming Options

## 🎯 The Naming Challenge
We must balance **Industry Branding** (attracting students with "AI") against **Transfer Compatibility** (ensuring CSU Fresno and other institutions recognize the course content for articulation).

---

## 🏛️ Option 1: The "AI-Forward" Approach
*Focuses on maximum marketability and clear program identity.*

| Level | Proposed Title | Pros | Cons |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Program** | AI-Skilled Web Developer | High industry appeal; clear modern brand. | May feel "trendy" to some committees. |
| **CIT 82** | Intro to AI & Web Development | Accurate to new content. | None. |
| **CIT 93** | AI-Augmented JavaScript | Signals modern workflow (TDD/AI). | **High risk** for CSU articulation. |
| **CIT 94** | AI Web Workflows | Perfect for a capstone. | None. |

---

## 🏗️ Option 2: The "Functional/Modern" Approach
*Focuses on long-term stability while implying modern methods.*

| Level | Proposed Title | Pros | Cons |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Program** | Modern Web Development | Sounds professional and permanent. | "Modern" is a relative term. |
| **CIT 82** | Web Development Foundations | Very safe for transfer. | Doesn't highlight the AI shift. |
| **CIT 93** | JavaScript Programming | Maximum transfer compatibility. | Doesn't signal the new TDD focus. |
| **CIT 94** | Full-Stack Web Workflows | Professional and descriptive. | A bit generic. |

---

## 🤝 Option 3: The "Hybrid" Approach (Recommended)
*Keeps the AI branding at the Program/Capstone level but protects the transfer core.*

| Level | Proposed Title | Pros | Cons |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Program** | **AI-Skilled Web Developer** | **Strong Brand.** | None. |
| **CIT 82** | Intro to AI & Web Development | Bold entry point. | None. |
| **CIT 93** | **JavaScript** | **Safest for CSU Transfer.** | Standard title. |
| **CIT 94** | **AI Web Workflows** | **Strong Capstone.** | None. |

---

## 🗣️ Discussion Questions for the Team
1. **The "AI" Shelf Life:** Do we believe "AI" will be a standard part of the title in 5 years, or is it better to keep it in the *description*?
2. **Transfer Friction:** If we change the name of CIT 93, are we prepared to re-submit for articulation with CSU Fresno?
3. **Student Perception:** Which title would a student be more likely to click on in the college catalog?
